THE PROBLEM

Colony Collapse Disorder (CCD) is decimating bee populations worldwide, with devastating consequences for ecosystems and agriculture

According to the United Nations, about 40% of all bee colonies collapse every year. In Africa, 60% collapse every year, and it is no different in Kenya. The collapse of bee colonies across the globe, also known as colony collapse disorder (CCD), is a major environmental issue with significant implications for ecosystems, agriculture, and food security.


Bees are essential pollinators, responsible for pollinating a significant portion of the world's crops, including fruits, vegetables, nuts, and seeds. According to the United Nations, with honeybees responsible for pollinating 80% of our food, our future is dependent on their survival. In return, small-scale farmers lack bees to pollinate crops, leading to food insecurity. They pollinate everything: lettuce, apples, avocados, tomatoes, cucumbers, almonds, cotton, and coffee. We literally live on the stuff they pollinate.


In fact, out of 100 beekeepers interviewed by One Acre Fund, 75% attributed their low yields to a lack of pollination. According to a 2022 study by the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health published in Environmental Health Perspectives, the global loss of pollinators is affecting human health. The study noted that inadequate pollination had caused a three to five percent loss of fruit, vegetables, and nut production. The lower consumption of these foods means that about one percent of all deaths can now be attributed to pollinator loss.


There is a need to ensure bees do not die and help with precise pollination to guarantee better crop yields and overall food security. The loss of honeybees from the planet could have serious consequences for both the environment and the global food supply. As the primary pollinators of many crops, the decline or loss of honeybees could result in decreased yields and reduced crop diversity. This could lead to higher food prices and a potential food shortage.


The loss of honeybees would also impact other species that depend on them, including plants and animals that rely on pollinated crops for food. Additionally, the loss of honeybees would disrupt the delicate balance of ecosystems, leading to a cascade of ecological effects.

Our Innovative Solution

Pollen Patrollers is an innovative agritech company tackling the challenge of bee colony collapse using patented smart hive technology. Our IoT-enabled hives are equipped with sensors that monitor critical hive metrics such as temperature, humidity, sound, and foraging activity. This data is transmitted to cloud platforms, where AI and machine learning algorithms analyze it to generate real-time, actionable insights for beekeepers. These insights improve hive health, minimize losses, and ultimately boost honey production and income for small-scale beekeepers.


Beyond hive monitoring, Pollen Patrollers support small-scale farmers with precision pollination services. We partner with beekeepers to deliver healthy bee colonies to farms during peak bloom periods. Using advanced mapping tools and satellite imagery, we identify the optimal placement of hives on each farm. AI-driven algorithms analyze farm data—such as crop type, bloom schedules, weather, and colony availability—to create efficient pollination plans. Continuous monitoring ensures that bee activity is tracked and adjusted as needed, maximizing crop yields and quality.


Pollen Patrollers also prioritizes education and inclusivity. We use AI-powered hotlines and USSD chatbots to train farmers in sustainable agriculture, even in rural areas with limited internet access. These tools offer real-time support in local languages, helping farmers adopt better practices in pest control, soil health, and water use.


As the first company to deploy this full-stack pollination and hive technology in Sub-Saharan Africa, Pollen Patrollers is making significant strides in climate resilience, food security, and economic empowerment. By combining IoT, AI, and farmer-focused training tools, we are bridging the gap between technology and smallholder farming, making agriculture smarter, more sustainable, and more inclusive.

DERISKING OUR CONSTRAINT

THROUGH THE YEGRO GRANT AWARD


The YEGRO award will enable us to unlock our key constraint by funding the expansion of our #Patrollers4Her program, which trains young women in IoT, diagnostics, repair, and data support for smart hives and precision pollination devices. This support will allow us to build a pool of skilled youth technicians essential for scaling our operations sustainably. The funds will go towards developing a structured training curriculum, organizing technical workshops, and facilitating hands-on learning.


In addition, we will design and deploy a digital training platform powered by Generative AI that delivers the curriculum both online and offline, ensuring inclusive access regardless of internet connectivity. The AI-powered digital platform will be a mobile-first, offline-capable system that delivers interactive training, mentorship, and technical support to youth. It will use generative AI to create personalized learning content, provide real-time chatbot assistance, and offer troubleshooting guides in local languages. Users can access training videos, quizzes, and resources even without internet. The platform will track progress and issue digital certificates upon completion.


The platform will include a smart matching feature to connect trainees with mentors and peers, supporting continuous learning and job readiness in the growing IoT and Agri-tech sectors. Through this support, we aim to train 500 new youth trainees, equipping them with practical skills for meaningful employment.


By investing in this training model, we reduce our operational costs, improve device uptime, and create community ownership of our technology. Trainees will be equipped not only with technical skills but also entrepreneurial knowledge, enabling them to offer services beyond our network. This multiplier effect will support youth economic empowerment while ensuring reliable service delivery for our growing customer base. The platform and curriculum will be scalable, allowing us to expand nationally with consistency and impact.

Value Proposition

1. Curriculum Development (Month 1)

  • Co-create a practical, inclusive IoT training curriculum for youth, integrating smart hive and precision pollination technologies.
  • Ensure alignment with generative AI tools and offline-first access to improve learning in rural settings.

2. Platform Design & Launch (Month 1-2)

  • Develop and deploy a blended learning platform (online and offline) powered by AI to host the training modules.
  • Include real-time diagnostics, video tutorials, quizzes, and community forums for peer learning.

3. Recruitment & Training (Month 2-4)

  • Identify, onboard, and train 500 youth (majority female) through the #Patrollers4Her program.
  • Deliver hands-on training sessions on IoT maintenance, device repair, data handling, and soft skills for agricultural tech.

4. Deployment of Youth Technicians (Month 4-5)

  • Assign trained technicians to serve smallholder farmers and beekeepers using smart hive and pollination devices.
  • Provide tools and ongoing technical support to ensure success in the field.

5. Monitoring, Evaluation & Feedback (Month 5-6)

  • Track training outcomes, device efficiency improvements, and customer satisfaction.
  • Collect feedback from both technicians and end-users to refine the program and platform.

6. Final Reporting & Knowledge Sharing (End of Month 6)

  • Submit comprehensive impact report to AGRA, detailing job creation, gender inclusion, and revenue growth.
  • Share learnings and best practices through webinars and open-source documentation.

Creation of Work Opportunities for More Youth and Especially Women

This project will directly create 500 new work opportunities for youth, with a deliberate focus on training and empowering young women through our #Patrollers4Her initiative. By equipping them with technical skills in IoT maintenance, diagnostics, and data support, we position them as certified technicians able to install, service, and repair smart hives and precision pollination devices used by farmers and beekeepers across the country. We anticipate integrating 100 of these trained young women into the Pollen Patrollers workforce by the end of the grant period.

Secondly, Kenya's IoT space is rapidly growing, with an estimated market value of over USD 670 million in 2024 and increasing demand in agriculture, energy, and health. Those who are not absorbed directly into Pollen Patrollers' operations will receive certificates of completion and recommendation letters, enabling them to secure jobs within Kenya's expanding IoT ecosystem.
